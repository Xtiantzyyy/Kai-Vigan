ATTRACTIONS

VIGAN CITY HERITAGE HOUSES

The place is listed in the UNESCO World Heritage Site because of the preservation of almost 200 houses, and various structures that bring any tourist in the past era. Most of the old houses in the place are still used by the descendants of the original owners while others were converted into museums and galleries. Vigan is the best-preserved example of a planned Spanish colonial town in Asia.

SYQUIA MANSION MUSEUM

This ancestral home is where the late President Elpidio Quirino lived with his wife. The house belongs to his wife’s family. The ancestral house features high ceiling rooms and hard floors and wood carvings, antique wood furniture, and porcelain pieces. It displays the architecture and significant family heirlooms and artifacts as testimony to the affluence of the family during the Spanish colonial era in the 19th century Philippines.

VILLA ANGELA HERITAGE HOUSE

Visitors who want to experience the charm of old world Vigan, can seek accommodations at the Villa Angela Heritage House. Located along Quirino Boulevard, this house is typical of those constructed in Vigan in the 1800s. Agapito B. Florendo, a gobernadorcillo, had it built in 1870. The house is part 2,000 square meter property owned by the Versoza family. They named it after their matriarch, Angela Verzosa Villanueva.

CRISOLOSO MUSEUM

The century-old mansion turned into a museum is owned by Floro S. Crisologo. Floro S. Crisologo was the patriarch of the clan and a congressman known for being responsible for landmark legislations that not only benefited his constituents but the whole country as well.The tragic event in his life was the inspiration for opening the museum. The museum is managed by the Crisologo family and is well-maintained.

BALUARTE ZOO

It is a zoological park that aims to protect and conserve endangered species and wildlife. Baluarte offers a mini zoo, resort complex that lies in hectares of land, and a butterfly farm. The area open to the public has plenty more engaging attractions that visitors can busy themselves with aside from the mini zoo. There is a butterfly farm, a track for horse riding and a skeet shooting range.

MESTIZO RIVER

One of the rivers that surround Vigan City and has a great involvement in the history of the city and on the culture of Vigueños as well. During the pre colonial period, it was used by Asian traders in bringing their mercantile to the old city and sometimes, they used the river bank as trading post. When Juan de Salcedo, a young Spanish conquistador, explored the Northern Luzon, they passed through this river and landed on its bank.
