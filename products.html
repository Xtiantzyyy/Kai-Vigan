CRAFTS

Burnay Jar

Jars made from Vigan are much sought-after by foreign and local visitors. Its earlier use were for tea drinking, and as container for salt, brown sugar, water, local wine (basi) and bagoong (fermented fish). Nowadays, people buy them mostly to serve as decorations inside their homes and gardens.

Abel Iloco

The abel is the traditional woven product of Vigan and the Ilocos region that started since the early Spanish occupation and is still produced today. The abel cloth is known for being a strong, colorful material. hand-woven cloths that you can use as blankets, table runners, or display.

Antique Collection

Visitors to Vigan who would like to view the craftsmanship of real antiques and antique reproductions can visit the shops which specialize in them that are found along Calle Crisollogo and Plaridel Street. These include religious figurines, wall display, pen holder, and many more.

Inabel Weaving

Native to the Ilocano region of the Philippines, Inabel - also known as Abel - is a durable, colorful fabric made from yarns of cotton. The cotton is harvested, prepared into yarn and then dyed.
